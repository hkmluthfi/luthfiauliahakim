9 Days in Seoul: Medals, Markets, and Memories

Seoul International Invention Fair 2025

Welcome to my Seoul journal. This trip was a milestone—not just academically, but personally. It was my first time navigating the bustling, neon-lit streets of South Korea.

Day 1-3: The Setup & The Pitch

The first few days were a blur of jet lag and intense preparation. Setting up our booth for the agricultural digital platform at SIIF 2025 required meticulous attention to detail. Presenting our innovation to international judges was nerve-wracking, but relying on clear communication and solid data paid off.

Day 4-6: The Reward and Exploring the City

Winning the Bronze Medal was a surreal moment. It validated months of hard work. After the formal events concluded, it was time to observe the city. Walking through Gyeongbokgung Palace and contrasting it with the hyper-modern skyline of Gangnam offered a masterclass in urban planning and cultural preservation.

Day 7-9: Culinary Adventures and Farewells

You cannot visit Korea without diving into the food. From authentic Tteokbokki in Myeongdong to late-night K-BBQ, every meal was an experience. Leaving Seoul was bittersweet, but I carried back a medal and a completely renewed perspective on global innovation.

Cross-Border Learning: My Life at UTM Johor

Student Exchange Semester

Stepping into Universiti Teknologi Malaysia (UTM) as an exchange student was both exciting and challenging. The environment in Johor Bahru presented a different rhythm compared to Bandung.

Academically, the focus on practical applications in subjects like Terrestrial Laser Scanning and Photogrammetry broadened my technical toolkit. Culturally, collaborating with students from diverse backgrounds taught me invaluable lessons in adaptability and international teamwork. It wasn't just about transferring credits; it was about expanding my worldview.

Lessons from the Field: Patimban Port Internship

PT Anugerah Samudera Madanindo

Classrooms teach you the theory of precision; the field teaches you the reality of execution. My internship at the Patimban Port project was a crash course in large-scale marine construction.

Observing seawall profiling and Multibeam Echosounder (MBES) surveys from a vessel revealed the incredible logistical coordination required to manage massive infrastructure projects. As an observer by nature, I found beauty in how structured workflows overcome chaotic environmental variables like tides and weather.
