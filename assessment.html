Personality Profile

Moderate

Moderate Emotional Control relates for talents to a moderate ability to keep their emotions steady and under control in response to life experiences. Mainly positive emotions about past, present and future. Overall satisfaction with life. Moderate levels of stress resilience. Some ability to overcome setbacks and disappointments. Psychological resilience.

Strengths

  • Activities with protected risk and moderate challenge.
  • Problem solving.
  • Supporting others with lower emotional control.

Vulnerabilities

  • To be anxious in high risk situations.
  • To draw back from challenges that would stretch them, but they are capable of doing.
  • To be held back by others around them with higher levels of anxiety and lower resilience.
  • To underestimate their resilience under pressure.

Advice for Future Work

  • Must not let negative emotions hold them back from attempting new opportunities, such as self-employment.
  • Must be aware of the role they have in interfacing between colleagues with high and low emotional control to enable collaboration.
  • They could have a supportive role to colleagues who have lower level of emotional control.
  • Must be aware of their level of stress tolerance and choose environments where this will not be compromised.
  • Value their ability to pick up on risk.

Careers

Moderate Emotional Control is valuable in many careers where there are moderate levels of risk and uncertainty. This may include work where they can play a supportive role to senior leadership and management. Self-employment is also a potential career option.

Moderate

They relate closely to both their external and internal worlds and have characteristics of Extraversion and Introversion. Creative - Adventurous - Imaginative - Think of new ideas - Friendliness - Energetic - Excitement-seeking - Sociable – Outgoing - Take the lead - Content in their own company - A listener - Emotional regulation - Self-reflection.

Strengths

  • A wide range of work environments.
  • A balance between social and solo time.

Vulnerabilities

  • To need a balance between time on their own and time with other people.
  • To lose motivation, energy and concentration if the balance swings too far in one direction.

Advice for Future Work

  • Must be aware of when they need to spend time with others or time on their own.
  • Must have a strategy for what to do when they realise they need more interaction with either their external world or internal world to function at their best.

Careers

Moderate Extraverts have an important role in most careers because they can balance social contexts as well as independent work. They have a special role in interfacing between extravert and introvert colleagues to bring collaboration and unity of purpose.

Moderate

Moderate Openness indicates their ability to adapt to routines and their willingness to explore new ideas and experiences. Imaginative – Creative - Individualistic and non-conforming - Intellectually curious - Artistic interests – Adventurous - Broad interests - Express feeling readily - Down to earth – Conventional - Balance between familiarity and novelty - Conforming.

Strengths

  • Being open-minded to new ideas and experiences.
  • Learning new skills or knowledge.
  • Seeing things from different perspectives.
  • Completing tasks and following procedures.

Vulnerabilities

  • To be content to follow routines and established procedures and miss opportunities to gain new experiences and knowledge or contribute creative ideas in work.
  • To remain in a role that does not provide opportunities to be creative and explore new ideas and experiences.

Advice for Future Work

  • If they are mainly engaged in routine work, must find opportunities for creative activities as well.
  • Must take advantage of opportunities to enhance their present skills, knowledge and experience.
  • Must be willing to volunteer to take the lead in projects.
  • Must congratulate oneself when they step out of their comfort zone as it will build their confidence.

Careers

There is an important role for people who are moderate in Openness in most careers. The ability to pivot from creative thinking and new experiences to routine procedures and tasks gives a flexibility to thrive in most working environments from administration and management to creative design and software coding.

Moderate

Combines a balance of being agreeable and disagreeable, which means the talent is able to get on with others well but also to be more objective in relating to others when it is appropriate. Compliant - Kind - Trusting – Conciliatory – Considerate – Friendly - Generous - Helpful - Willingness to compromise to others’ interests - Optimistic view of people - Assertive – Sceptical – Confident - Honest.

Strengths

  • Working with and getting on with other people.
  • Understanding other’s point of view.
  • Actively listening to others.
  • Collaborating with colleagues.

Vulnerabilities

  • To fail to express their views.
  • To fail to make objective tough decisions.
  • To hide what they really think.
  • To avoid conflict when it is appropriate.
  • To be weak in bargaining and negotiating for their own interests.

Advice for Future Work

  • Must consider careers that are associated with people.
  • Be prepared to express your views when not in agreement.
  • Value your ability to represent others who are high in agreeableness and have difficulty expressing themselves.

Careers

Agreeableness is highly valued, especially in careers involving people, such as teaching and caring professions. It is a valuable trait in being part of a team and in leadership. Develop your weaker traits to widen your career choices.

High

Talent is strong in controlling and regulating his/her life and work. Dependability - Hard working - Self-efficacy - Orderliness - Dutifulness – Diligent Ambition - Striving for achievement - Sacrificing gratification for a goal - Self-discipline - Persistence.

Strengths

  • Setting goals and striving to achieve them.
  • Organising themselves and others to achieve an objective.
  • Hard and challenging work.
  • Being considered reliable in a fast-paced environment.

Vulnerabilities

  • To be prone to stress and driven to achieve unreachable goals.
  • To place unrealistic demands on others around them, especially if they do not match your level of Conscientiousness.

Advice for Future Work

  • Must look for work that is challenging and allows them to aim for and achieve goals.
  • Be conscientious about taking breaks from work to do things they enjoy for their own sake.

Careers

High Conscientiousness is valued in most careers and by most employers and is a valuable trait for recruitment and promotion. It is a characteristic that is valued in senior management in organisations, as well as a key trait in entrepreneurs.

Learning Style

High

Learners with a preference for the combined Visual and Kinesthetic style grasp concepts more effectively through direct interaction with learning materials. They thrive on engaging physically with tasks and benefit from the use of visual aids.

Examples of Application

  • Engaging with interactive simulations and physical models.
  • Using diagrams, flowcharts, and illustrative videos to understand complex concepts.
  • Participating in workshops where they can physically manipulate relevant materials.
Moderate

Aural learners absorb information best when it is presented verbally. They excel in environments where listening and discussion are encouraged and are adept at remembering spoken instructions.

Examples of Application

  • Benefiting from lectures, group discussions, and verbal briefings.
  • Using podcasts and audio recordings for learning new content.
  • Participating in study groups or team meetings where ideas are discussed aloud.
High

Read / Write learners prefer to interact with information through written words. They excel in traditional study methods involving reading and taking detailed notes.

Examples of Application

  • Using textbooks, articles, and written handouts as primary study materials.
  • Making comprehensive lists, writing out notes, and summarizing information.
  • Preferring email and text-based communication for clarity and record-keeping.
